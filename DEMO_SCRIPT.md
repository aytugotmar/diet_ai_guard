## 🎬 Demo Script

### [0:00 - 0:20] Opening Hook (20 seconds)

### [0:20 - 1:30] Live Demo (70 seconds)

**[Open browser to localhost:3000]**

> "Here's how it works. Four simple inputs."

**[Fill destination]**
> "Where do you want to go? Let's say... Paris."

**Type**: `Paris, France`

**[Fill dates]**

> "I'm feeling foodie. Watch this."

**[Point to loading screen]**

> "While it works, it keeps you engaged with travel facts."

> "Every activity has estimated costs. No surprises."

**[Scroll to bottom]**

> "And travel tips personalized for Paris."

## 🎓 Handling Q&A

### Expected Questions & Answers

**Q: How accurate is the AI?**
> "We use Google Gemini 1.5 Pro, trained on millions of travel guides. We also validate against real booking data. Accuracy improves with every generation."

**Q: How do you make money?**
> "Freemium model - free users get 3 itineraries monthly. Premium is $9.99 for unlimited. We can also add booking commissions from hotels and tours."

**Q: What about real-time weather?**
> "Great question. Current version simulates it, but integrating a weather API like OpenWeather is a one-line change. We focused on the AI logic first."

**Q: Why not use ChatGPT?**
> "Gemini 1.5 Pro has a larger context window and better structured output. Perfect for detailed itineraries. But we're model-agnostic - could swap easily."

**Q: How do you find hidden gems?**
> "We train the AI with prompts emphasizing 'non-touristy' and 'where locals go'. We also plan to integrate with local bloggers and Reddit threads."

**Q: Is this mobile responsive?**
> "Absolutely. Try it on your phone right now. Built mobile-first with Tailwind CSS."

**Q: What's your go-to-market strategy?**
> "Launch with SEO-optimized landing pages for top destinations. Partner with travel influencers for Instagram promotion. Freemium converts 3-5% to paid."

**Q: How long did this take?**
> "We built this in 48 hours for this hackathon. That's the power of modern frameworks and AI APIs."

**Q: Can it handle group travel?**
> "Not yet, but V2 will support collaborative planning. Multiple people can vote on activities. Think of it as a 'travel party planner'."

**Q: What about accessibility?**
> "High contrast mode coming soon. Already screen-reader friendly with semantic HTML. WCAG AA compliance is our target."

## 🎨 Presentation Tips

### Body Language
- ✅ Stand confidently, shoulders back
- ✅ Make eye contact with judges
- ✅ Use hand gestures to emphasize
- ✅ Smile when showing cool features
- ✅ Move around stage (if space)

### Voice
- ✅ Speak clearly and slowly
- ✅ Vary tone to maintain interest
- ✅ Pause after key points
- ✅ Emphasize "AI", "hidden gems", "your vibe"
- ✅ Project confidence

### Pacing
- ✅ Don't rush the demo
- ✅ Let features breathe
- ✅ Pause for laughs/reactions
- ✅ Watch the clock
- ✅ Finish strong, not rushed

### What NOT to Do
- ❌ Apologize for bugs
- ❌ Say "This is just a prototype"
- ❌ Over-explain technical details
- ❌ Read from slides
- ❌ Turn your back to judges
- ❌ Go over time limit
